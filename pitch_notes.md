# Pitch text

[Problem] AMINA is a lean crypto bank; compliance has to scale by automation and risks have to be detected fast. The core gap is what a former compliance analyst at a peer digital-asset bank told us: a client's risk rating officially changes only at periodic review, once a year for high-risk, every five for low. Risk would be event-driven but reviews currently are not. She also said: somewhere between 80 and 95 percent of alerts she checks are false positives, and she has to re-judge every one by hand. This re-judging takes serious time and money.
[Solution] So we built DRIFTWATCH: perpetual KYC, not periodic. Every client is a golden record; public and internal signals suggest updates continuously, showing the drift score. We score the biggest risks, the way she actually judges a case, and we flag invalidated assumptions.
[Trace, into the demo] Take a low-risk Swiss SaaS client. Assume the registry shows a new shareholder who trips a PEP and sanctions screen, news reports the firm pivoting into a crypto OTC desk, and a dormant account starts moving high volume. Any one could be noise; but together they clearly cross a risk threshold. Watch it live!:
[Video, playthrough 1, the three users] The three lines of defence: the first-line managers who own the client, compliance that controls the risk, and the Money laundry risk officer who owns the regulatory call. For example the drift score recommends to change the risk from mediu to high.
[Playthrough 2, how they interact] Now watch them work it. The relationship manager in the morning sees the alert and decides to forward to compliance; compliance confirms and takes action. Thie then goes to operations or the money-laundry risk officer.
[Cost] And it's cheap by design: a cheap filter clears the noise, a mid model classifies survivors, the top model only builds case files for the few that reach an analyst. The technical admins can track the cost live.
[Compliance] And this is the part our analyst said is non-negotiable: we never reclassify anyone automatcially. A second perosn verifies and then draft a detailed ticket to operations; operations makes the change. Every flag links to its source document, so the analyst judges the evidence, not our score. That's built to pass FINMA, not just a demo.
[Thank you] Thank you. Questions?

# Pitch Notes

## Format

- 3 minute pitch; keep to time.
- Partners review the slides before the pitch.
- Winning teams will be invited (to the next round).
- Whole team should be on stage; keep to 2 people max actually pitching.

## Structure

- Start strong: practice with teammates, record yourself, or listen back.
- Cover: what you are solving, who the target is, why it matters, and how you do it.
- Present the team.
- End with a clear ask or takeaway.

## Slides

- Keep slides simple; not much text.
- Use simple, powerful language.
- Avoid putting anything on a slide that you do not say out loud.
- Recommended: show the solution in a short video.

## Q&A

- Have answers ready for likely questions.
