Helvetia SaaS GmbH faces regulatory probe into unregistered crypto OTC activity

By Markus Frei, Financial Correspondent · ZUG, Switzerland, 18 June 2026

Swiss authorities have opened an inquiry into Helvetia SaaS GmbH over what regulators describe as undisclosed over-the-counter crypto brokerage and derivatives activity carried out without the required licences, two people familiar with the matter said.

The Zug-based company, long known as a business-to-business software vendor, is alleged to have begun routing large crypto OTC trades and structured derivatives through a newly added offshore shareholding layer over the past two quarters. Investigators are examining whether those flows were properly registered and whether counterparties were screened.

"The concern is a business-model shift that outran the firm's regulatory permissions," said a person briefed on the review.

According to commercial register filings reviewed by this service, a new beneficial owner holding more than ten percent of the company was recorded through a British Virgin Islands nominee structure. The change coincided with the stated pivot from recurring software revenue toward crypto trading desks.

Helvetia SaaS GmbH did not respond to requests for comment. The regulator declined to confirm or deny the existence of any investigation, citing its standing policy on ongoing matters. No charges have been filed, and an inquiry does not imply wrongdoing.

Compliance specialists note that an abrupt move into crypto OTC and derivatives, combined with a new opaque ownership layer, is the kind of profile drift that typically triggers a fresh know-your-customer review at correspondent banks.
